Air Pollution:

Air pollution refers to the presence of harmful substances in the air, which can have detrimental effects on human health and the environment. Common pollutants include carbon monoxide (CO), sulfur dioxide (SO2), nitrogen oxides (NOx), ozone (O3), particulate matter (PM), and volatile organic compounds (VOCs).

Sources of Air Pollution

  • Transportation: Cars, trucks, and airplanes release harmful gases into the atmosphere.
  • Industrial Activities: Factories and power plants emit large quantities of pollutants such as CO2 and sulfur dioxide.
  • Agricultural Activities: Livestock and pesticide use can contribute to air pollution.
  • Residential Activities: Burning wood, coal, or other fuels for heating also releases harmful pollutants.

Effects of Air Pollution

Air pollution is linked to a variety of health problems, such as asthma, respiratory infections, cardiovascular diseases, and even premature death. In addition to human health, it contributes to environmental issues such as global warming and acid rain.
